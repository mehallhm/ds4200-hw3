Welcome!

Hello! I'm Michael Mehall, a second year Data Science and Economics student studying at Northeastern University. In the world of computer science, I love building developer tooling and infrastructure, creating the foundation for successful software. Currently I am involved in the software consultancy group Sandbox on campus developing and maintaining the SearchNEU project, which is used and loved by over twenty five thousand students across all of Northeastern's campuses. In addition, I founded Altitude Web Design which delivers high quality custom software solutions and consulting to companies of all size. Outside of academics, I'm an avid mountain biker, skier, climber, and general enjoyer of the outdoors (this picture from Northeastern's home mtb race in western Massachusetts).

Some of my latest projects include building a document database with a Google Doc as a storage layer, recreating a relational database from scratch, as well as building a Docker deployment tool to help ease the burden of self hosting applications. The majority of these projects are built in the Go programming language as it is both extremely versatile but also simple, helping keep abstraction costs down and forcing the developer to be explicit when programming, avoiding technical debt often incurred through "clever" programming.
