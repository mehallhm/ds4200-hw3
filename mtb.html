Cycling

I like bikes. I guess that should be apparent from what I said on the home page about me being an avid mountain biker among the other things... but being outside is truly a unique experience. I have raced mountain bikes for the last 4 years, joining my high school team (yes, our high school had a mountain biking team, it was awesome) and having two pretty great seasons. I then came to college and joined Northeastern's team where I have now raced for one season (took a year off the bike my Freshman year). Part of the team is pictured below at our home race!

The latest of my escapades outside have actually been climbing. A friend dragged me out to a gym one day and it respired my love for the sport, and thus I spent the last summer outside enjoying the walls. I am blessed enough to have a father who was thrilled to get back into a sport he did in his youth who taught me a ton (literally life saving amount) of the important stuff. Climbing outside, especially "traditional" climbing where you place your own protection, has a lot of skill and technique involved in it that can be tricky to learn when a mistake costs your life.
